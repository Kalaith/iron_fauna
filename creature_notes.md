My one addition: "Power"

I actually like the name Power, but I'd define it as the creature's biological capacity rather than a combat stat.

For example:

Species

Fox

Power: 40

Speed: 95

Size: Small

Limbs: 4

Type: Mammal
Bear

Power: 90

Speed: 40

Size: Large

Limbs: 6

Type: Mammal

Power determines things like:

Maximum weapon weight
Maximum armor weight
Total Vigor pool
Maximum strain before overload
Limb regrowth speed
Ability to wield heavy graftware

That creates a nice balance where:

A rabbit might carry one excellent rifle and dance around the battlefield.
A bear can haul a cannon, shield, and mortar but becomes a slow-moving fortress.
A spider can mount six utility devices but lacks the raw Power to fire all of them continuously.
One more thought

Rather than thinking in terms of 57 different creatures, I'd think in terms of 57 different chassis.

Players won't collect a "Fox" because it has Quick Attack. They'll collect it because it's the fastest medium Power chassis in the game, perfect for hit-and-run electrical builds.

That's a niche that doesn't become obsolete when you find a stronger creature later. A fast scout, a heavy siege beast, a support healer, and an all-rounder can all remain relevant because they fill different engineering roles rather than sitting on a simple linear power curve.
